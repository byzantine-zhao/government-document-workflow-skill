# Operational Government Work Plans

Use this reference when drafting or revising work plans that must be executable across government departments, especially social stability, neighbor-effect/NIMBY, major project, emergency response, petition handling, public participation, or cross-department coordination plans.

## Anti-Empty-Plan Rule

Do not leave measures at the level of slogans. Each key measure should identify:

- Lead department
- Support departments
- Territorial government or responsible unit
- Work product or deliverable
- Time limit or trigger condition
- Reporting path
- Closure or acceptance standard

Replace vague wording:

- "加强组织领导" -> name the leadership group, office, meeting frequency, and reporting path.
- "强化部门协同" -> name the lead unit and support units by task.
- "做好宣传引导" -> define spokesperson, release channel, review process, response time, and rumor-handling path.
- "完善应急处置" -> define response level, arrival time, command structure, traffic control, medical support, evidence preservation, and after-action review.

## Public-Rule Alignment Checklist

When precision matters, compare the plan against public central/provincial/municipal/district rules and templates. For mainland China, common public-rule anchors include:

- Major administrative decision procedure: public participation, expert consultation, risk assessment, legality review, collective discussion.
- Environmental impact assessment public participation: information disclosure, public comments, explanation of technical issues, monitoring disclosure.
- Petition and complaint handling: registration, assignment, time limit, feedback, review, and follow-up.
- Emergency plan management: risk assessment, response levels, command system, drills, reporting, recovery, review.
- Public finance and procurement rules: budget source, purchase of third-party services, performance management, audit trail.

State the comparison as operational requirements, not legal conclusions. Recommend local legal/compliance review before issuance.

## Organization Structure Pattern

For city or district work plans, use this structure when appropriate:

1. Leadership group: group leader, deputy leaders, member units.
2. Office: host department, office director, deputy directors, daily coordination duties.
3. Special working groups:
   - Comprehensive coordination
   - Project assessment
   - Social impact assessment
   - Public communication and petition handling
   - Technical review
   - Traffic support
   - Publicity and online public opinion
   - On-site response
   - Funding support
   - Supervision and accountability
4. Project-level task force: one project, one task force, one risk assessment, one response plan, one issue ledger.

Avoid naming a department as responsible for a matter outside its normal portfolio. For example, traffic organization belongs with transportation/public security traffic police/urban management; legality review belongs with justice/legal affairs; public opinion monitoring belongs with cyber/publicity offices; petition closure belongs with petition departments and territorial governments.

## Responsibility Matrix Pattern

Use a responsibility matrix in prose or an appendix:

- Project list owner: development and reform department or industry主管部门.
- Territorial stability owner: county/district government.
- Industry policy owner: project主管部门.
- Environmental monitoring owner: ecology and environment department.
- Site order owner: territorial government and public security.
- Traffic support owner: transportation department, traffic police, urban management.
- Petition owner: petition department and territorial government.
- Public communications owner: publicity office, cyber office, project主管部门.
- Funding owner: finance department, project主管部门, construction/operation unit.
- Supervision owner: government office, political-legal committee, petition department, development and reform department.

## Social Impact And Risk Assessment

For neighbor-effect/NIMBY, major infrastructure, public utility, waste treatment, transport, energy, health, welfare, or funeral projects, include a social impact assessment path:

- Define affected area: residential communities, schools, hospitals, eldercare, businesses, road nodes, villages, sensitive facilities.
- Establish baseline: population, vulnerable groups, property/land interests, commuting, employment, public service access, community identity, previous disputes.
- Identify impacts: environment, health perception, traffic, construction disturbance, property expectations, public service burden, trust in government, online sentiment.
- Compare alternatives: site, route, technology, scale, construction period, operation mode.
- Define mitigation: monitoring, disclosure, compensation or benefit-sharing where lawful, traffic diversion, buffer design, community facilities, complaint channels.
- Require third-party review when disputes are major or technical trust is low.
- Include post-evaluation after start, trial operation, and formal operation.

## Traffic, Funding, And Support Details

Traffic support should specify:

- Construction vehicle routes and time windows.
- Temporary road occupation approval and notice.
- School/hospital/market peak-hour measures.
- Emergency lanes and ambulance/fire access.
- Public transport adjustment or shuttle service.
- Parking and crowd dispersal plans.
- Signage, volunteers, and traffic police deployment.

Funding support should specify:

- Risk assessment and third-party service costs.
- Public participation, hearing, expert review, interpretation, and publicity costs.
- Environmental and safety monitoring costs.
- Emergency drills and on-site response costs.
- Traffic support and temporary facility costs.
- Lawful relief, support, or community improvement measures.
- Budget source, procurement path, performance target, and audit record.

## Response And Closure

Define graded response triggers:

- Blue/yellow/orange/red project risk levels or Level IV/III/II/I emergency response if local rules use that structure.
- Who starts the response.
- Arrival time for on-site group.
- Time to initial public response.
- Time to written report.
- Escalation path to city leadership.

Closure should require evidence:

- Risk point resolved or controlled.
- Public explanation completed.
- Monitoring data disclosed.
- Petition items answered.
- Traffic or site measures implemented.
- Responsible leader and lead department sign off.
- Post-evaluation date scheduled.
