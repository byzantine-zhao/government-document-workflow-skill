# Document Types

Use this reference for drafting, rewriting, or choosing a Chinese administrative document type. Mainland party and government organs commonly use 15 official document types under the 2012 party/government document handling rules: 决议、决定、命令（令）、公报、公告、通告、意见、通知、通报、报告、请示、批复、议案、函、纪要.

## Type Selection Rules

- Use 决议 for major decision matters deliberated and adopted by meetings.
- Use 决定 for important decisions and deployments, rewards/punishments, or changing/canceling inappropriate lower-level decisions.
- Use 命令（令） for publishing administrative regulations/rules, announcing major compulsory measures, approving ranks, or rewarding relevant units/personnel within statutory authority.
- Use 公报 to publish important decisions or major matters.
- Use 公告 for important or statutory matters announced domestically and internationally.
- Use 通告 for matters to be observed or known within a certain scope.
- Use 意见 to present views and handling measures on important issues.
- Use 请示 only when asking a superior authority for instruction or approval.
- Use 报告 only to report work, reflect situations, or answer inquiries; do not request approval in a report.
- Use 函 for non-subordinate bodies or equal-level consultation, inquiry, reply, request for approval, or answer to approval matters.
- Use 通知 for deployment, transmission, forwarding, meeting arrangement, or matters requiring awareness/execution.
- Use 批复 to answer a lower-level request for instructions.
- Use 通报 to commend, criticize, communicate important spirit, or inform important situations.
- Use 议案 for matters submitted by governments to people's congresses or their standing committees under legal procedure.
- Use 纪要 to record main meeting situations and agreed matters.

## Resolution, Order, Communique, Announcement, Public Notice

Use these less common document types only when the issuing body has the corresponding authority and procedure:

- 决议: major matters adopted by a formal meeting.
- 命令（令）: statutory command instrument; requires clear legal authority.
- 公报: public release of important decisions or major matters.
- 公告: important or statutory matters released domestically and internationally.
- 通告: matters to be observed or known within a defined scope.

Avoid:
- Using command-like types for ordinary office deployment.
- Using public-facing types before disclosure, confidentiality, and personal-information review.

## Notice

Use for deploying work, communicating requirements, forwarding documents, arranging meetings, or informing matters within the sender's authority.

Structure:
- Basis or background
- Main task or arrangement
- Requirements, responsible units, deadlines
- Contact or attachment note when needed

Avoid:
- Asking a superior for approval
- Reporting completed work as the main purpose
- Giving orders beyond the sender's authority

## Opinion

Use for important issues that need views, policy positions, handling measures, or implementation guidance.

Structure:
- Background and basis
- Overall requirements or principles
- Main measures
- Division of responsibilities
- Implementation requirements

Avoid:
- Writing broad slogans without implementable measures
- Using opinion when a binding order, approval, or report is required

## Request For Instructions

Use when asking a superior authority to approve, decide, or give instructions on a specific matter.

Structure:
- Reason and background
- Policy/legal/institutional basis
- Specific request, preferably one main matter
- Proposed solution when appropriate
- Closing phrase such as "妥否，请批示"

Avoid:
- Mixing several unrelated requests
- Sending to multiple superior authorities without a clear main recipient
- Using "报告" when approval is requested

## Report

Use when reporting work, reflecting situations, replying to superior inquiries, or submitting recommendations without requesting approval.

Structure:
- Work background
- Main facts and progress
- Problems and analysis
- Next steps or recommendations

Avoid:
- Ending with approval language
- Concealing unresolved risks
- Turning a report into a disguised request for instructions

## Notification Of Criticism Or Commendation

Use 通报 to commend advanced examples, criticize errors, convey important spirit, or inform important situations.

Structure:
- Basic facts
- Nature and significance or problem analysis
- Decision or handling opinion when applicable
- Lessons, requirements, and next steps

Avoid:
- Publicly naming people or organizations beyond necessity
- Including disciplinary conclusions without authority or procedure

## Reply

Use when answering a request from a lower-level body.

Structure:
- Reference to the received request
- Clear approval, partial approval, or disapproval
- Conditions, requirements, or implementation instructions
- Accountability and reporting requirements when needed

Avoid:
- Ambiguous statements such as "原则同意" without conditions
- Approving matters outside authority

## Decision

Use 决定 for important decisions and deployments, rewards/punishments, or changing/canceling inappropriate lower-level decisions.

Structure:
- Basis and reason
- Decision items
- Implementation requirements
- Effective date or transition arrangement when needed

Avoid:
- Using decision for routine notice matters
- Changing rights/obligations without legal or policy basis

## Proposal

Use 议案 for governments submitting matters to the people's congress or its standing committee under legal procedure.

Avoid using 议案 for internal administrative proposals or ordinary work plans.

## Letter

Use between bodies without direct superior-subordinate relationship for consultation, inquiry, reply, approval request between equal-level or non-affiliated bodies, or business contact.

Structure:
- Matter background
- Specific consultation/request/reply
- Deadline or cooperation requirements
- Polite administrative closing

Avoid:
- Command tone toward non-subordinate bodies
- Vague asks without deliverables

## Meeting Minutes

Use to record agreed matters from a meeting and guide implementation.

Structure:
- Meeting time, place, host, attendees, topic
- Main discussion points
- Decisions and responsible units
- Deadlines and follow-up mechanism

Avoid:
- Attributing decisions to absent leaders
- Recording unconfirmed opinions as final decisions

## Announcement And Public Notice

Use for public-facing information within legal authority.

Structure:
- Legal or policy basis
- Public matter
- Scope, period, requirements, channels
- Effective date and contact path

Avoid:
- Publishing personal or sensitive information without necessity
- Using internal shorthand that citizens cannot understand
